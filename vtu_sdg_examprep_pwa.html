What are the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)?
Answer
The SDGs are 17 global goals adopted by the UN in September 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda. They address poverty, hunger, health, education, climate change, and peace — to be achieved by 2030, replacing the earlier MDGs.
